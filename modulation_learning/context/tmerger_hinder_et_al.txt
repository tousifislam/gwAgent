\section*{Fit for Time to merger from Hinder et al 2017}

We now determine $\Delta t = t_{\rm peak} - t_{\rm ref}$ given the parameters at

$t_{\rm ref}$. The most general functional form would be

\begin{equation}

    \Delta t(q, e, l) = \sum_{ijk=0}^{\infty} a_{ijk}\, q^{i} e^{j} \cos(kl - \alpha_{ijk})

    \label{eq:7}

\end{equation}

where we use a Taylor expansion in $q$ and $e$, and a Fourier series in $l$, since

$l$ is a periodic variable. In order to match the NR data, we find that we require

quadratic terms in $q$ and $e$, but only the first mode in $l$. Since there can be

no variation with $l$ when $e = 0$, we must have $a_{i01} = 0$. The resulting

model for $\Delta t$ is

\begin{equation}

\begin{aligned}

    \Delta t(q, e, l) = \Delta t_0 + a_1 e + a_2 e^2 + b_1 q + b_2 q^2 + \\

    c_1 e \cos(l + c_2) + c_3 e q

\end{aligned}

    \label{eq:8}

\end{equation}

There are 8 unknown parameters, and the model is fitted to all 23 simulations.

The fitted function is

\begin{equation}

\begin{aligned}

    \Delta t(q, e, l) = 391.196 + 3.13391\,e - 2492.95\,e^2 + \\

    2.77212\,q - 17.92\,eq + 8.11842\,q^2 + \\

    76.4944\,e\cos(0.626653 + l)

\end{aligned}

    \label{eq:9}

\end{equation}
